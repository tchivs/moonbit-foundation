<feature>
  <name>Bounded deterministic FixedOrStored PNG encoding</name>
  <files>modules/mb-image/png/png.mbt, modules/mb-image/png/encode.mbt, modules/mb-image/png/stream_encode.mbt, modules/mb-image/png/encode_test.mbt, modules/mb-image/png/encode_wbtest.mbt, modules/mb-image/png/stream_encode_test.mbt, modules/mb-image/png/stream_encode_wbtest.mbt</files>
  <behavior>
    - FixedOrStored construction completes all source capability, checked geometry, exact selected-length, exact logical-work, disposition, and budget work before an eager Writer or chunk output lease can receive a byte.
    - The private A1 matcher evaluates distances 1, 2, 3, and 4 in ascending order, compares no more than 258 remaining filter-None scanline bytes, accepts matches of length at least 3, chooses the longest match, and preserves the first equal-length distance as the deterministic tie break.
    - The private A2 ledger records every deterministic planning lookup/comparison and selected emission work; fixed replay must reproduce planned matcher work and reports a private state error on mismatch.
    - Fixed output counts exact DEFLATE bits through BFINAL/BTYPE, literals or length-distance tokens, EOB, and byte padding; it is selected exactly when its complete PNG total is no larger than the stored total. The selected IDAT length must fit the PNG four-byte chunk field.
    - A fixed byte is previewed but not committed by present. Only an acknowledgement after the eager Writer accepts exactly one byte or the chunk lease accepts the byte advances fixed cursor, bit state, Adler, IDAT CRC, and total progress.
    - Existing eager/chunk factories, outcomes, private machine constructor, and semantic interface remain intact. Legacy constructors retain explicit Stored selection and their frozen stored bytes.
  </behavior>
  <implementation>Use the existing PngCompressionStrategy and configured factories from Phase 32. Reuse the decoder's private bit-reversal/fixed-code precedent and the existing PNG CRC/Adler helpers; do not add a second PNG formatter, a token list, compressed payload buffer, scanline buffer, retained caller lease, dependency, FFI codec, or public declaration. Phase 34 alone owns corpus fixtures, benchmark reporting, measured compression claims, and its evidence presentation.</implementation>
</feature>
