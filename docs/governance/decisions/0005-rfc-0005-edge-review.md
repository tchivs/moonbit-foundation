# Edge Review Record: RFC 0005 (mb-text)

> **Historical record.** The edge-review process this file records was a mandatory precondition of the `sole-project-owner-bootstrap` acceptance route, which was removed from the [RFC process](../../rfcs/README.md) on 2026-07-23. This edge review is retained because it is a genuine architectural review of RFC 0005 — it found the UAX#29/bidi ownership overlap with `mb-core/unicode` and prompted the §2/§4.1/§5 fix that made mb-text consume mb-core/unicode rather than redefine it. It is **no longer a mandatory acceptance precondition** — no acceptance route exists in the simplified process. The "Acceptance-route note" below should be read as a description of the now-removed machinery, not a live requirement.

## Edge review results

- `EDGE-GOV-01-UNCLASSIFIED`: **Completed. Disposition: no-omission-found (after charter revision).** Scope reviewed: module identity (`tchivs/mb-text`); the two downward dependency edges declared in §2 (mb-text -> `mb-font`, mb-text -> `mb-core`); the text-vs-font boundary (§3.1); the text-vs-rasterization boundary (§3.2 — mb-text produces positioned glyph *references*, not pixels); the text-vs-document-structure boundary (§3.3 — paragraph-level scope vs document-level concerns); the Unicode-boundary relationship to `mb-core/unicode` (revised §2 / §4.1 / §5); the portable platform seam (§5); and the v0.x subset with binding deferral. Result: after the charter revision documented below, no material architectural omission or blocking objection is found — mb-text correctly consumes `mb-core/unicode` under RFC 0001 §6.4 rather than redefining it, and the text/font/rasterization/document boundaries are coherent. Non-blocking observations (5), lead with the resolved UAX#29 finding:
  1. **UAX#29 / bidi-class ownership overlap with `mb-core/unicode` — RESOLVED by charter revision.** This was the review's strongest finding and the only blocking-objection candidate. RFC 0005 §4.1 as originally written claimed mb-text OWNS "Unicode segmentation per UAX#29" and §5 said it "ships its own bounded Unicode property tables," while `mb-core/unicode` is an already-accepted public package whose `pkg.generated.mbti` exports `segment_graphemes`, `should_break_grapheme`, `grapheme_break_class` (UAX#29 grapheme), `bidi_class` (`BidiClass`), `general_category` (`GeneralCategory`), `is_digit`, `is_letter`, `is_whitespace`, and `unicode_version()`. That was a direct responsibility overlap and a potential silent redefinition of mb-core's accepted public Unicode boundary, which under RFC 0001 §11 would itself require an RFC. **This was fixed** by the §2 / §4.1 / §5 charter revision recorded below: mb-text now consumes mb-core/unicode's UAX#29 segmentation and BidiClass contracts rather than re-implementing or duplicating them. After revision the overlap is gone and the candidate blocking objection is withdrawn.
  2. **Shaping engine choice undecided (consumer contract underspecified).** §11 leaves the exact OpenType feature-set boundary between "basic" and "full" shaping undecided, and §6.1 says only "at minimum liga, cmap, basic kern." No portable shaping algorithm or engine is named: HarfBuzz is rejected as a host dependency (§8) but no portable replacement is identified. This leaves the downstream consumer contract for shaping underspecified; a cleanly deferred, explicitly-named portable target would be preferable. Non-blocking; recommend nailing down before implementation begins.
  3. **Bidi algorithm effectively pre-6.3.** §6.1 defers FSI/LRI/RLI/PDI isolate handling, which has been normative since Unicode 6.3. The RFC does not state a named UAX#9 revision target, so the conformance level of bidi output is ambiguous; this also interacts with §11's unpinned Unicode version. Non-blocking; recommend stating the targeted UAX#9 conformance level explicitly.
  4. **UAX#14 line-break version unversioned.** The line-break property set is not tied to a specific UAX#14 revision, and ZWJ/emoji line-break interactions are not addressed. Non-blocking; recommend pinning a version.
  5. **Minor internal inconsistency in edge rationale.** §2 states that the mb-text -> mb-font edge includes "outline access," but §3.2 routes outline access through the *consumer* (not through mb-text). This is a mild mismatch in the edge's stated rationale rather than a boundary defect. Minor.

- `EDGE-GOV-02-UNCLASSIFIED`: **Completed. Disposition: no-omission-found.** Scope reviewed: RFC 0005 status (Proposed); header honesty (no route claimed, no approvals recorded, no evidence attached, review open); lifecycle ledger (only `-> Draft -> Proposed`); fail-closed behavior under rfc-process §8; header/ledger/index agreement. Result: no omitted transition or authority case; the header honestly claims nothing it does not have, and the recorded ledger matches the index. **Non-blocking forward-looking finding (authority gap the acceptance step must close):** the sole-project-owner bootstrap (§4.3 / Decision 0001) was explicitly scoped "RFC 0001 only" and its preauthorization is consumed (`AUTH-NO-LATER-APPROVAL`), so RFC 0005 cannot reuse it. The RFC 0005 header correctly states "no route claimed" and "public review window not used" — which forecloses the §4.2 public-review route as currently stated, and §4.1 (two-maintainer approval) is structurally unavailable under the current single-maintainer roster. **Therefore, under the current maintainer roster plus the "public review not used" header, NO acceptance route is currently available to RFC 0005.** This is not a defect of the Proposed header (it claims nothing falsely); it is the authority gap that any future acceptance step must close.

- Unresolved blocking objections: **none.** The UAX#29 / bidi-class overlap with `mb-core/unicode` was the only blocking-objection candidate; it is resolved by the charter revision documented below.

## Charter revisions prompted by this review

- **RFC 0005 §2 (dependency-edge prose):** clarified that the mb-text -> mb-core edge specifically *consumes* `mb-core/unicode`'s UAX#29 segmentation and bidi-class contracts. mb-text does not re-implement, duplicate tables, or redefine mb-core's public Unicode boundary. Any text-layout-specific Unicode need not present in `mb-core/unicode` (e.g. UAX#14 line-break properties, UAX#9 isolate resolution beyond `bidi_class`) is layered on top as a consumer.
- **RFC 0005 §4.1 (owned responsibilities):** "Unicode segmentation" changed from owned to **consumer-not-owner** — grapheme/word/sentence segmentation is owned by `mb-core/unicode` and consumed by mb-text; mb-text owns only text-layout-specific segmentation layered on top. "Bidirectional algorithm" clarified and split: raw `BidiClass` classification is owned by `mb-core/unicode` and consumed, while the bidi *algorithm* (paragraph level, embedding, resolution) is owned by mb-text and layered on those classes.
- **RFC 0005 §5 (tables):** "ships its own bounded Unicode property tables" changed to "consumes `mb-core/unicode`'s already-shipped tables and adds only supplementary tables `mb-core/unicode` does not yet expose (UAX#14 line-break categories, UAX#9 mirroring pairs) as a consumer layered on `mb-core/unicode`, without duplicating or forking lower-layer tables."

After this revision, the potential blocking objection (direct responsibility overlap / silent redefinition of `mb-core/unicode`'s accepted public Unicode boundary, which under RFC 0001 §11 would have required its own RFC) is **resolved**: mb-text now correctly consumes `mb-core/unicode` under RFC 0001 §6.4 rather than redefining it.

## Acceptance-route note (does not advance the RFC)

This record does not advance RFC 0005; it remains Proposed. Recording the two mandatory edge reviews while Proposed is permitted and is the prerequisite for any later acceptance, but no acceptance route is currently available to RFC 0005 under the present facts. The sole-project-owner bootstrap (§4.3 / Decision 0001) was scoped "RFC 0001 only" and its preauthorization is consumed, so it cannot be reused. The RFC 0005 header honestly records "no route claimed" and "public review window not used," which forecloses the §4.2 route as stated, and §4.1 (two-maintainer approval) is structurally unavailable under the current single-maintainer roster. To advance, RFC 0005 must close this authority gap by one of: (a) opening a real ≥7-day public-review window under §4.2 plus project-lead approval, (b) adding a second maintainer so §4.1 becomes available, or (c) producing a new sole-owner decision artifact explicitly scoped to RFC 0005. None of these is a defect in the Proposed header; they are the conditions the acceptance step must satisfy.
